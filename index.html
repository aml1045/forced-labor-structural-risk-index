The Framework

Human trafficking is not random violence. It is a structural system enabled by specific, measurable conditions.

What is Forced Labor?

Forced labor occurs when people are coerced into work through threat, deception, or exploitation of vulnerability. Human trafficking is the mechanism: recruiters identify vulnerable people, move them into exploitative situations, extract labor value, and retain proceeds.

The ILO estimates 28 million people are in forced labor globally. But raw numbers obscure the real question: what structural conditions enable trafficking to persist at scale?

28M
People in forced labor

Why Structural Analysis?

Criminal interventions alone cannot end trafficking. Traffickers replace arrested operatives; victims are re-victimized. Prevention requires understanding the structural conditions that make trafficking possible.

Behavioral Approach
Focus on individual traffickers
Action: arrest, prosecute, convict
Limitation: operatives are replaceable
Necessary but insufficient
Structural Approach
Focus on systemic conditions
Action: reduce vulnerability, strengthen institutions
Strength: addresses root causes
Required for prevention

The Self-Reinforcing Loop

Trafficking is not linear. It is a cycle that perpetuates itself. Each phase creates the conditions for the next.

Shocks, displacement, and weak institutions create exposed populations.

The Three Phases

The FLSRI measures structural risk across three interconnected phases of the trafficking lifecycle.

Phase 1: Recruitment

Where traffickers gain access to victims

Structural conditions that generate exposed, immobile, or coercible populations.

Key Mechanisms
  • Structural Disruption
  • Displacement & Transit
  • Reduced Bargaining Power
  • Institutional Protection Deficit
4 domains · 15 indicators

Phase 2: Exploitation

Where labor is extracted under coercion

Conditions that suppress resistance and enable sustained control of trafficked workers.

Key Mechanisms
  • Enforcement Capacity
  • Concealment Geography
  • Sector Exposure
  • Institutional Coercion Enablement
  • Structural Entrapment
5 domains · 15 indicators

Phase 3: Monetization

Where proceeds are retained and reinvested

Structural conditions that enable value extraction and financial concealment.

Key Mechanisms
  • Financial Architecture Opacity
  • Laundering Capacity
  • Cash & Informal Reliance
  • Financial Infrastructure Access
4 domains · 6 indicators

The Composite Index

The FLSRI combines all three phases into a single structural risk score. Because trafficking requires conditions across all phases, the final score penalizes imbalance—a country cannot score high unless risk exists throughout the loop.

The Reinforcement Loop & Composite Index

The trafficking lifecycle is a self-reinforcing loop. Exploitation and monetization infrastructure regenerate the structural conditions that enable recruitment. This page shows how the feedback mechanism works and the final composite scores.

Feedback Formula: R_adj = R x max(0.50, (E x M)^0.2)
FLSRI = (max(0.05, R_adj) x max(0.05, E) x max(0.05, M))^(1/3)
